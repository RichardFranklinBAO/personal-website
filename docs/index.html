About Me

I am a graduating master’s student in Biostatistics at Emory University and an incoming Ph.D. student in Statistics at the University of South Carolina (August 2026), where I will pursue research at the intersection of statistical machine learning, time-series analysis, and quantitative finance.

My current research spans two domains. At Emory, I work as a Research Assistant under Professor Jingjing Yang, developing deep learning models (LSTM/BiLSTM) to predict Alzheimer’s disease neuropathology trajectories using longitudinal cohort data from the ROS/MAP study. I also collaborate with Dr. Ramesh Manyam and Dr. Sheena Warner on a machine learning pipeline to predict severe maternal morbidity (SMM) using data from the nuMoM2b cohort, applying ensemble methods including Random Forest, XGBoost, and LightGBM with class imbalance correction via SMOTE.

Outside of academia, I am interested in quantitative trading, options market microstructure, and the application of statistical methods to financial time series.
